Developed Countries - Why are they called developed?

A developed country is defined as a sovereign state that has a developed economy and technologically advanced infrastructure when compared to other nations. Several factors that determine whether or not a country is developed, such as the Gross National Income, Health care spend per capita, Life Expectancy and freedom are used to evaluate and rank countries.

We will be reviewing the data starting 1990 for 14 countries. As you analyze the data, a developed is ranked higher based on how good it is with respect to other countries for that evaluation parameter We are evalulating today 2 parameters that define a Developed country
1) Gross National Income (Per Capita) - Higher the number, more money each citizen in the country has and more properous they will be.
2) Life Expectancy at Birth - Higher the number, longer the life span of the citizen, which in turn means they have better health overall.
